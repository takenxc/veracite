Global Summary

The Middle East continues to dominate global attention. Iran and Israel exchanged direct strikes across a two-week period in April 2026, drawing urgent calls from the UN Security Council and threatening to widen the conflict into a regional war.

In Gaza, the humanitarian situation has reached catastrophic levels with international aid agencies reporting famine conditions across the northern strip. Ceasefire talks in Cairo collapsed for the third time this year.

On the technological front, autonomous AI systems are reshaping critical infrastructure, raising urgent questions about accountability and oversight that legislators are only beginning to address.

In Europe, Germany finally formed a governing coalition after 14 weeks of negotiations, with major implications for EU defence spending and Ukraine aid.

Historical Event Timeline

Notable moments that shaped the modern world

2026
Iran–Israel direct conflict
Mutual strikes mark the first direct, acknowledged military exchange between the two nations, threatening wider regional escalation.
2024
Global election super-cycle
Over 60 nations held national elections — the largest single-year democratic exercise in history, reshaping governments across every continent.
2023
Hamas attacks & Gaza war begins
The October 7 Hamas attacks on Israel and the subsequent Israeli military campaign in Gaza triggered the most devastating conflict in the region in decades.
2022
Russia invades Ukraine
The largest land war in Europe since 1945 destabilised global energy markets, displaced millions, and fractured the post-Cold War security order.
2020
COVID-19 pandemic
The WHO declared a global pandemic on 11 March 2020. Over 7 million deaths were recorded before widespread vaccination campaigns contained transmission.
2015
Paris Climate Agreement
195 nations committed to limiting warming to 1.5–2°C above pre-industrial levels in the most significant multilateral climate accord in history.
2011
Arab Spring
A wave of pro-democracy uprisings swept North Africa and the Middle East, toppling governments in Tunisia, Egypt, and Libya and reshaping regional politics.
2008
Global financial crisis
The collapse of Lehman Brothers triggered the worst financial crisis since 1929, producing recessions across the developed world and years of austerity.
2001
September 11 attacks
Al-Qaeda's attacks on New York and Washington launched two decades of US military engagement in the Middle East and transformed global security policy.
1994
Rwandan Genocide
Approximately 800,000 Tutsi and moderate Hutu were killed in 100 days. The international community's failure to intervene remains a defining indictment of global institutions.
1991
Dissolution of the Soviet Union
15 independent states emerged from the USSR's collapse, ending the Cold War and reconfiguring global power for the following three decades.
1948
Founding of Israel & first Arab–Israeli war
The declaration of Israeli statehood and the immediate war with neighbouring Arab states set the foundations for a conflict still unresolved today.
1945
End of World War II & UN founded
The deadliest conflict in history ended with the defeat of Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan. The United Nations was established to prevent future global war.
1914
World War I begins
The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand triggered a cascade of alliances, resulting in four years of industrialised warfare and 20 million deaths.
