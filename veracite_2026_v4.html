About Veracite

Veracite was founded in 2026 with a simple premise: young adults deserve a news source that respects their intelligence. Most news is either overwhelming in volume or shallow in context. We do neither.

Every article includes 2–3 months of background so you understand why something matters, not just that it happened. Unfamiliar terms are defined inside the article. We cover conflict, politics, science, technology, culture, and sport — because the world doesn't fit into one category.

We have no advertisers. No political affiliation. No agenda beyond accuracy.

Global Summary

The Middle East is at its most volatile in decades. Iran and Israel exchanged direct military strikes in April — the first open acknowledged exchange between the two nations — raising the spectre of a wider regional war. In Gaza, the death toll has passed 50,000 and famine conditions have been confirmed in the north, while ceasefire talks hosted in Cairo collapsed for the third time this year. Separately, Sudan's civil war entered its third year with 10 million displaced — one of the world's worst humanitarian crises, largely ignored by international media.

In geopolitics, the US–China trade war escalated sharply, with tariffs reaching 145% on key goods including semiconductors and pharmaceuticals, fracturing supply chains that took decades to build. Germany finally formed a governing coalition after 14 weeks of deadlock, committing to historic defence spending increases. Ukraine ceasefire talks reopened under US mediation for the first time in over three years, though a deal remains distant.

In science and technology, China landed the first humans on Mars on 2 April, watched by 2.4 billion people — a milestone that reshuffled the global space race overnight. AI now autonomously manages national power grids in three countries, while a gene therapy cure for sickle cell disease passed Phase 3 clinical trials, offering hope to 8 million sufferers worldwide.

On climate and society, three Pacific island nations face total inundation within 15 years as global temperatures hold above 1.62°C. The WHO declared a global youth mental health emergency, citing a 42% rise in adolescent depression since 2019. And the 2026 FIFA World Cup is underway across North America, with Morocco and Japan producing the tournament's biggest upsets so far.

Historical Event Timeline

Major events across conflict, science, culture, politics, sport, and exploration — from antiquity to 2026

2026
War
Iran–Israel direct military exchange
The first open, acknowledged military exchange between Iran and Israel raises fears of a wider regional war involving Lebanon, Iraq, and Gulf states.
2026
Science
First humans land on Mars
China's Tianwen-3 mission lands three taikonauts on the Martian surface on 2 April — the first crewed interplanetary landing in human history.
2025
Politics
G7 AI governance frameworks adopted
The world's leading economies agreed binding rules on AI transparency, safety testing, and labelling of AI-generated content for the first time.
2024
War
Global election super-cycle
Over 60 nations held national elections, affecting nearly 4 billion voters. Populist and far-right movements gained ground across Europe, Asia, and Latin America.
2024
Sport
Paris Olympic Games
France hosted the Summer Olympics for the third time. The opening ceremony held on the Seine river was watched by an estimated 1.5 billion people globally.
2024
Science
First gene-edited pig kidney transplanted into a living human
Surgeons in Boston transplanted a genetically modified pig kidney into a patient, keeping them alive for 47 days — a landmark in xenotransplantation research.
2023
War
Hamas attacks Israel — Gaza war begins
The 7 October Hamas attack killed ~1,200 Israelis. Israel's military response became one of the most destructive urban campaigns in modern history.
2023
Culture
Hollywood strikes — writers and actors halt production
The WGA and SAG-AFTRA strikes paralysed the US entertainment industry for months, with AI's role in creative work at the centre of negotiations.
2022
Science
James Webb Space Telescope first images released
NASA released the deepest infrared images of the universe ever captured, showing galaxies over 13 billion light years away and reshaping cosmological research.
2022
War
Russia invades Ukraine
The largest land war in Europe since 1945 triggered a global energy and food crisis. NATO expanded to include Finland and Sweden for the first time.
2022
Sport
Qatar FIFA World Cup — Argentina win
Lionel Messi finally won the World Cup in one of the most dramatic finals in history, defeating France on penalties. Widely regarded as the greatest World Cup final ever played.
2022
Culture
Death of Queen Elizabeth II
Queen Elizabeth II died on 8 September 2022 after 70 years on the throne — the longest reign in British history. King Charles III was crowned in 2023.
2021
Politics
US Capitol stormed by rioters
Supporters of President Trump stormed the US Capitol building on 6 January, attempting to halt the certification of the 2020 election result. Five people died.
2021
Science
COVID-19 vaccines rolled out globally
Multiple vaccines developed in under a year — the fastest in history — began global rollout. mRNA technology, used by Pfizer and Moderna, opened a new era in vaccine development.
2020
Politics
COVID-19 pandemic declared
The WHO declared a global pandemic on 11 March 2020. Over 7 million deaths were recorded. Lockdowns, border closures, and economic collapse reshaped every aspect of daily life.
2020
Culture
George Floyd killed — global protests erupt
The murder of George Floyd by a Minneapolis police officer sparked the largest civil rights protests in the United States since the 1960s, spreading to over 60 countries.
2020
Economy
Global recession — worst since 1929
COVID-19 caused the sharpest economic contraction in nearly a century. GDP fell across every major economy simultaneously for the first time in recorded history.
2019
Science
First image of a black hole captured
The Event Horizon Telescope collaboration released the first direct image of a black hole — in galaxy M87 — confirming predictions of Einstein's general theory of relativity.
2016
Politics
Brexit vote & Trump elected
The UK voted 52-48% to leave the European Union. Weeks later, Donald Trump was elected US President — two events that defined a decade of Western political upheaval.
2016
Science
Gravitational waves detected for the first time
LIGO scientists announced the first direct detection of gravitational waves — ripples in spacetime caused by colliding black holes, confirming another prediction of Einstein's general relativity.
2015
Politics
Paris Climate Agreement signed
195 nations agreed to limit global warming to 1.5–2°C above pre-industrial levels — the most significant multilateral climate accord in history.
2012
Science
Higgs boson particle discovered
CERN announced the discovery of the Higgs boson at the Large Hadron Collider — confirming the final missing piece of the Standard Model of particle physics.
2011
Culture
Arab Spring
Mass uprisings swept North Africa and the Middle East, toppling governments in Tunisia, Egypt, and Libya. The movement sparked hope for democratic change that largely went unrealised.
2008
Economy
Global financial crisis
The collapse of Lehman Brothers triggered the worst financial crisis since 1929, producing recessions across the developed world and a decade of austerity in Europe.
2008
Politics
Barack Obama elected US President
Obama became the first African-American President of the United States, winning on a platform of hope and change against the backdrop of two ongoing wars and a financial crisis.
2007
Science
iPhone launched — the smartphone era begins
Apple's Steve Jobs unveiled the first iPhone in January 2007, fundamentally changing human communication, media consumption, commerce, and social interaction worldwide.
2003
War
US-led invasion of Iraq
Coalition forces invaded Iraq, toppling Saddam Hussein. No weapons of mass destruction were found. The resulting power vacuum contributed directly to the rise of ISIS.
2001
War
September 11 attacks
Al-Qaeda's attacks killed nearly 3,000 people and launched two decades of US military engagement in the Middle East, reshaping global security, travel, and civil liberties.
2000
Science
Human genome sequenced
Scientists announced the first draft of the complete human genome — 3 billion DNA base pairs mapped — opening the door to personalised medicine and genetic research.
2000
Economy
Dot-com bubble bursts
The collapse of overvalued internet companies wiped out $5 trillion in market value. Many companies vanished; survivors like Amazon and Google reshaped the global economy.
2000
Sport
Sydney Olympics — Cathy Freeman lights the flame
Australia's Sydney Olympics are widely regarded as the greatest modern Games. Indigenous Australian Cathy Freeman lit the cauldron and won gold in the 400m — a defining moment of reconciliation.
1997
Politics
Hong Kong returned to China
The UK handed Hong Kong back to China on 1 July 1997 after 156 years of colonial rule, under a "one country, two systems" framework that has since been substantially eroded.
1994
War
Rwandan Genocide
Approximately 800,000 Tutsi and moderate Hutu were killed in 100 days. The international community's failure to intervene remains a defining indictment of global institutions.
1994
Sport
Nelson Mandela opens the Football World Cup in the USA
The first World Cup held in the United States coincided with South Africa's first democratic elections. Brazil won a record fourth title. The event marked football's arrival as a truly global sport in North America.
1991
Politics
Dissolution of the Soviet Union
15 independent nations emerged from the USSR's collapse, ending the Cold War and creating a brief unipolar world order dominated by the United States.
1991
Culture
World Wide Web made publicly available
Tim Berners-Lee opened the World Wide Web to the public, beginning the most rapid transformation of human communication and commerce since the printing press.
1989
Politics
Berlin Wall falls — Cold War ends
The fall of the Berlin Wall on 9 November 1989 symbolised the collapse of Soviet-style communism in Eastern Europe. Germany was reunified in 1990.
1989
Culture
Tiananmen Square massacre
Chinese troops killed hundreds of pro-democracy protesters in Beijing's Tiananmen Square on 4 June. The event remains censored in China and symbolises the limits of political reform under the Communist Party.
1986
Sport
Maradona's "Hand of God"
Diego Maradona scored one of the most controversial — and greatest — goals in football history in the 1986 World Cup quarter-final against England. Argentina won the tournament.
1983
Science
HIV/AIDS identified
Scientists identified the HIV virus as the cause of AIDS. The epidemic went on to kill over 40 million people. The crisis transformed global understanding of public health and stigma.
1979
Politics
Iranian Revolution
The Shah of Iran was overthrown and replaced by an Islamic Republic under Ayatollah Khomeini — fundamentally reshaping Middle Eastern geopolitics in ways that reverberate to this day.
1969
Science
Moon landing — Apollo 11
Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin became the first humans to walk on the moon on 20 July 1969, watched live by an estimated 600 million people — then a third of the world's population.
1969
Culture
Woodstock music festival
400,000 people gathered on a farm in upstate New York for a three-day music festival that became the defining symbol of the 1960s counterculture movement.
1963
Politics
Assassination of JFK
US President John F. Kennedy was assassinated in Dallas on 22 November 1963 — an event that shattered American optimism and whose circumstances remain debated today.
1963
Culture
Martin Luther King Jr.'s "I Have a Dream" speech
King delivered his landmark address to 250,000 people at the Lincoln Memorial in Washington D.C. during the March on Washington — a turning point in the American civil rights movement.
1950
War
Korean War begins
North Korea invaded South Korea in June 1950, drawing in US-led UN forces and Chinese troops. The war ended in a 1953 armistice — technically never a formal peace — that defines the Korean Peninsula today.
1948
Politics
Founding of Israel & the Nakba
Israel declared statehood on 14 May 1948. The subsequent war displaced ~700,000 Palestinians — the Nakba, or 'catastrophe' — laying the foundation for a conflict unresolved to this day.
1947
Politics
India and Pakistan independence
The partition of British India created two independent states, triggering one of history's largest mass migrations and intercommunal violence that killed up to 2 million people.
1945
War
World War II ends — UN founded
The defeat of Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan ended the deadliest conflict in history. The United Nations was established to provide a framework for international peace.
1945
Science
Atomic bomb dropped on Hiroshima and Nagasaki
The US dropped nuclear bombs on two Japanese cities on 6 and 9 August, killing over 200,000 people and inaugurating the atomic age — and the decades-long threat of nuclear war.
1939
War
World War II begins
Germany invaded Poland on 1 September 1939, triggering declarations of war from Britain and France. Over six years, the conflict killed an estimated 70–85 million people — the deadliest in human history.
1929
Economy
Wall Street Crash — Great Depression begins
The US stock market crashed in October 1929, triggering a decade-long global depression, mass unemployment, and political instability that contributed to the rise of fascism in Europe.
1914
War
World War I begins
The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand triggered cascading alliances. Four years of industrialised warfare killed 20 million people and dismantled four empires.
1903
Culture
Wright Brothers — first powered flight
Orville Wright flew 37 metres at Kitty Hawk, North Carolina on 17 December 1903. Within 66 years, humans had reached the moon. Aviation became the backbone of global trade and travel.
1895
Science
X-rays discovered by Wilhelm Röntgen
Röntgen's accidental discovery of X-ray radiation transformed medicine, enabling doctors to see inside the human body without surgery for the first time.
1859
Science
Darwin publishes On the Origin of Species
Charles Darwin's theory of natural selection fundamentally changed humanity's understanding of life on Earth. It remains the foundational framework of modern biology.
1760
Economy
Industrial Revolution begins in Britain
Steam-powered machinery, factories, and mass production transformed British — and then global — society from agrarian to industrial. It created modern capitalism and urban civilisation as we know it.
1789
Politics
French Revolution begins
The storming of the Bastille on 14 July 1789 ended the French monarchy and introduced the modern concepts of liberty, equality, and popular sovereignty that underpin most democracies today.
1776
Politics
American Declaration of Independence
The thirteen American colonies declared independence from Britain on 4 July 1776, establishing a republic founded on Enlightenment principles that inspired democratic movements worldwide.
1440
Science
Gutenberg printing press invented
Johannes Gutenberg's movable-type press democratised access to knowledge, enabling the Protestant Reformation, the Scientific Revolution, and the Renaissance — arguably the most consequential invention in history.
1347
Science
The Black Death reaches Europe
Bubonic plague killed one-third of Europe's population between 1347 and 1351 — approximately 25 million people. It accelerated the collapse of feudalism and transformed European society.
